    QuiCK

    As part of my work in ACS Applied Research Team, I was the TL of QuiCK - a queuing system built for managing asynchronous tasks in CloudKit, Apple’s storage backend service.

    It was a great experience working on QuiCK and bringing it from idea to production at scale. We went through multiple iterations, and had to overcome many challenges (technical and non-technical ).

    ZooNet

    I received this project during my internship at Yahoo! Research. The initial goal was to design and implement a way to work with multiple instances of ZooKeeper while maintaining the semantics.

    I started by examining ZooKeeper and learning its internals. We identified possible improvements in ZooKeeper’s performance and contributed them to the project (here) along with a system test that generates corresponding workloads (here).

    For the construction of multiple instances, we proposed the modular composition approach, which is a client-side solution that I really like (actually, we later formally proved the construction’s correctness).

    Weighted Moves

    On my first internship at Google, I received a task to design a new load-balancing algorithm for Slicer - Google’s internal Sharding-As-A-Service system, that will replace an existing variant of consistent hashing that was in use.

    We designed a generic algorithm that we named “Weighted Moves” - the idea was to iteratively evaluate a few types of small load changes (i.e., “moves”) with respect to a user-defined cost function, and to make progress as long as there’s an allowance for them.

    In retrospect, I think that we designed a variant of gradient descent without thinking about it in that perspective
