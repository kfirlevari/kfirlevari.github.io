    Chosen Student Work

    Mixus

    I thought that it could be a win-win scenario if public places like pubs, stores, malls, buses, etc… will adjust their playlist according to the preferences of the people they serve at any given moment.

    I gave this idea to Daniel Mittelman and Tomer Brosh, and they went above and beyond and created “Mixus” - a proximity-based music platform based on users’ personal preferences.

    Dare You Go

    The vision I had for this project is to create a platform that will allow users to sign into challenges and earn prizes when proving they managed to overcome these challenges. I thought it can be nice to propose challenges among friends, family, or participate in such challenges and earn prizes as part of advertising campaigns.

    During that project, a movie called Nerve was released, explaining why this is actually not such a great idea to have a daring social platform

    This project was an effort of two teams - Owais Musa & Muhammad Watad worked on the backend, and Baraa Hleihil & Khaled Fahoum worked on the frontend. It was a pleasure seeing how well they organize, divide, and coordinate the work among themselves.

    Navigation by Touch

    It seems like a different lifetime, but back in those days when the smartwatches were new products, it seemed like no one really knew what do to with them

    I thought that we could use this new smart device to make navigation more accessible, either for people who can’t hear or see the navigation instructions or for those who prefer not to (i.e., during driving, walking, cycling, etc…). The idea was to translate navigation instruction from the smartphone to touch vibrations in the watch.

    I guess it doesn’t sound that exciting since nowadays this feature is already implemented in all smartwatches

    I had two great and independent teams, Lihi Yahav & Alexander Isenshtat worked on a version for Pebble watch, and Omar Massarweh & Hamam Massarweh worked on aversion for Android watch.
