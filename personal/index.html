    Education & Experience

    I received my BSc (Software Engineering) in 2010 and Ph.D. (Distributed Systems) in 2017, both from the Technion - Israel Institute of Technology. My Ph.D. advisor was Prof. Idit Keidar. In my research, I focused on distributed systems, parallel computing, and correctness of concurrent algorithms. Here is an article (in Hebrew) about my wife and me after we finished our Ph.D.

    Nowadays, I work at Apple, in Apple Cloud Services (ACS) CloudML team, where I’m a Senior Software Engineer. Before that, I was a Senior Software Engineer and a Technical Lead in ACS Applied Research team, led by Alex Shraer.

    During my Ph.D., I interned twice at Google - the first time in the Storage Analytics group and the second time in Google Cloud. In addition, I was an intern in Yahoo! Research. Before my Ph.D., I was a Software Engineer at Wanova, an Israeli startup company acquired by VMWare.

    Running

    I first learned about long-distance running during my bachelor’s degree as part of a course I took in this running style. Although I enjoyed it back then, I couldn’t find a way to balance running with other goals I had, so I didn’t run regularly during my Ph.D.

    After my Ph.D., I came back to running, and I was taken over by it. Generally, it means that I no longer know if I’m running to stay healthy or trying to stay healthy to run

    Here are some running books that I enjoyed. They either helped me find what running means to me or helped me shape the way I run -

    • Born to Run: A Hidden Tribe, Superathletes, and the Greatest Race the World Has Never Seen / Christopher McDougall here
    • Eat and Run: My Unlikely Journey to Ultramarathon Greatness / Scott Jurek here
    • The Lost Art of Running: A Journey to Rediscover the Forgotten Essence of Human Movement / Shane Benzie here
    • The Oxygen Advantage: Simple, Scientifically Proven Breathing Techniques to Help You Become Healthier, Slimmer, Faster, and Fitter You / Patrick G. McKeown here

    Yoga & Mindfulness

    Today, when I think about it, practicing yoga or practicing mindfulness is doing the same thing, just with different body parts.

    Mindfulness

    My journey with mindfulness started at an early age, when a friend of mine saw me at middle school reading a philosophy book and suggested I read meditation books instead, saying that they are “practical philosophy” books. So the first book on that topic that I read was “The Healing Power of Mind: Simple Meditation Exercises for Health, Well-Being, and Enlightenment” / Tulku Thondup here. I liked it, and I have started searching for good books and teachers ever since.

    Here are some of the teachers that influenced me the most along the way:

    • Jiddu Krishnamurti here
    • Viktor Frankl here
    • Eckhart Tolle here
    • Neale Donald Walsch here

    Yoga

    My early attempts with yoga left a bad impression on me, so I thought it was not meant for me. Then came the crazy 2020… I felt like it was a good time to give yoga another try, and indeed, it worked

    The first (online) teacher that made yoga accessible and joyful for me was Patrick Beach here. At some point, I gave DownDogApp here a try, and I’m hooked to it since.

    Photography

    Nowadays, photography for me is less of a hobby and mostly taking the iPhone out and capturing the kids doing amusing things , but I remember a time that photography was much more than that! (also weighted more! a DSLR camera isn’t great fun to walk with for a couple of hours…).

    Cityscape Photography

    When I photograph a mostly-static cityscape, I like to take a few moments and focus on the angle, level, lines, objects, etc… so that eventually, the resulting picture will echo the feeling I had during my visit to that place.

    Street Photography

    Street photography always excites me. Each session feels like a hunt for random, unique, and unrepeatable moments usually overlooked. I started experimenting with this style after becoming aware of Thomas Leuthard’s work (here) and reading his two ebooks “Going Candid” and “Collecting Souls”.
